🌿 A Brief History (2018-2021)

HackerFarm is the one and only of its kind, as India's pioneering camping-style residential environment that uniquely blended technology, nature, and holistic living. Promoting radical inclusion and self-reliance, it provided a space where people could join camps and programs to build meaningful social projects while collaborating with like-minded individuals.

The initiative was inspired by Jaaga Farm, taking particular inspiration from Freeman Murray's colearning initiative around farms in Bangalore, and aligned itself with Burning Man's 10 principles of community and self-expression. HackerFarm ran at farms around Nelamangala. Tumkur and Mysore (Karnataka).

The core philosophy centered around the belief that the Internet could help people learn and build sustainable livelihoods, supported by a physical ecosystem in rural-themed spaces like farms and city outskirts. Participants were encouraged to shape healthy life habits around structured routines while maintaining both offline and online community connections. The space welcomed people from all domains to explore life possibilities and develop survival skills for both present challenges and future opportunities.
